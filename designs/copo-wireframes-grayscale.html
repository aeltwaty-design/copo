Copo — Hi-Fi Wireframes

formerly Udealz · KSA coupons · grayscale, layout-focused

The full journey from onboarding to a successful payment, built around the new core idea: every store is a 4-level coupon journey. You enter at Level 1 with its coupons open; Levels 2–4 are visible but locked behind bigger discounts. Buy 2 coupons in a level to unlock the next (a level with a single coupon needs just that one). Pure grayscale — hierarchy comes from weight, fill and borders, not color.

04

Store screen — the 4-level coupon journey · core concept

The heart of Copo. Enter at Level 1 with its coupons open; Levels 2–4 sit right below, visible but locked behind bigger discounts. A journey track at the top always shows where you are and what's next. Buy 2 coupons in a level → the next unlocks. Below: the default (many coupons), the locked-level and add-to-cart interactions, then the three other cases — one-per-level, all-open, and just-leveled-up.

Why this layout?
Store — turn browsing into a journey.
  1. 1Store header. Cover carousel (1/4), back & save, logo, name + verified, rating · distance · open — trust before the offer.
  2. 2Level journey track. The whole 4-step arc at a glance: where you are (Level 1), what each level is worth (15→60%), and the one clear task — “buy 2 to unlock.” Locked steps build aspiration; the progress line drives it.
  3. 3Tabs. Coupons stays default; deeper info (how to use, details, branches) never buries the deals.
  4. 4Level 1 — OPEN. A labelled group with its discount ceiling; here it holds many coupons, each a compact row (title · deal vs original · % off · +add).
  5. 5Level 2 — LOCKED. Shown immediately below as a blurred preview under a lock + the exact unlock rule, so the payoff is visible but gated.
Interactions & other cases: tap a locked level (4b), add-to-cart sheet (4c), progress after buying (4d), one-per-level (4e), all-open (4f), just-leveled-up (4g).

Why this layout?
Checkout — settle it fast, no surprises.
  1. 1Order recap first. The coupons being bought, itemized, so there's no “what am I paying for?” at the last step.
